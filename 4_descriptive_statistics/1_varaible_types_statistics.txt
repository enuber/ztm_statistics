Variable Types in Statistics

Quantitative
any metric that measures or quantifies falls into the quantitative continuous bucket.
there are two types of quantitative variables
1. Continuous - have a continuous range like Age, Income, or Height
2. Discrete - these are grouped or catergorized like Age Groups [0-18], [18-25], Income Groups [10k-20k]

Qualitative
there are two types of qualitative
1. Nominal - eye color, room types, something that doesn't have a natural order
2. Ordinal - Education, Survey Data (like satisfied through unsatisfied), something that does have a natural order like for education there is elementary school, jr high school, high school then college. you can't just go to college without having gone through the rest.
